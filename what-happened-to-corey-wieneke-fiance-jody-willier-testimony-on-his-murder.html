Fiance Jody Willier's Testimony On Corey Wieneke's Murder

Fiance Jody Willier testified in March 2019 that her boyfriend, Corey Wieneke, was "a lot of fun to be around." She indicated that she was aware that he was having relationships with other women, but she still loved him.

"I overlooked a lot of stuff, and absorbed a lot of that," she said of Wieneke's affairs, according to The Muscatine Journal.

Annette Cahill, one of Wieneke's girlfriends, is charged with first-degree murder in his death in October 1992. She was arrested after a witness claimed she overheard Cahill confessing to killing Wieneke, a bartender, shortly after his death.

The recollection was subsequently revealed to be from an unknown lady who was nine years old at the time of Wieneke's murder. It is thought to comprise the majority of the prosecution's case.

Jessica Becker, now 36, allegedly told police that when she was nine, she went to play at Cahill's niece's home, in 1992. She says she can remember seeing Cahill pacing around, lighting candles, and making incriminating statements about murdering Wieneke. 

"Corey, I'm sorry for murdering you," Becker told the jury she overheard Cahill remark in a dark room of Cahill's home as Becker was spending the night with friends.

Cahill's attorneys had refuted the claims, insisting they're based on animosity rather than truth. Furthermore, the defense said that the confession was not real since it was overheard by a youngster who was not being examined by a qualified professional at the time.

According to the Muscatine Journal, a judge declared a mistrial in 2019 after a jury of five women and seven men could not find any physical evidence on her behalf.
