Age and Early Life of Pierce Brosnan

On May 16, 1953, Pierce Brosnan was born in Drogheda, County Louth, Ireland. Currently 67 years old, the actor. The well-known actor is the sole child of May and Thomas Brosnan, a carpenter. For 12 years, Brosnan lived in Navan, County Meath.

When Brosnan was a young child, his father left the household. His mother moved to London to pursue a career as a nurse and abandoned him in search of a better life.

The actor moved home with his aunt when his grandparents passed away before transferring to a boarding house. When he was 11 years old, he met up with his mother and her husband, William Carmichael, in London.

The actor went on to study commercial illustration at Saint Martin School of Art after completing his education at Elliot School. He enrolled in Drama Center London as a teenager because he wanted to be an actor, and he eventually earned his degree there in 1975.

Professional Career

After receiving his degree, the actor started his performing career at the York Theatre Royal as an acting assistant stage manager.

Additionally, he started acting in plays like “The Red Devil Battery Sign” and continued doing so until he got other roles in major motion pictures.

He started making cameo appearances on television and in movies in the 1970s and early 1980s. The actor afterward moved to the US to pursue a lucrative career, where he won his first important part in the five-season 1982 television drama “Remington Steele.”

After this series, he became well-known and was cast in The Fourth Protocol, an espionage movie from the “Cold War” series. He starred as Phileas Fogg in the 1989 television miniseries “Around the World in 80 Days,” which was broadcast in three parts.

As he had already established himself, he had several appearances in movies in the early 1990s, such as “Murder 1010” in 1991 and “Death Train” in 1993. The actor appeared in a supporting capacity in the comedy “Mrs. Doubtfire” in 1993.
